Engine C v2.0 — What Changed And Why

Engine A is unchanged and remains the only prediction engine in this system. Its Stage/Zone/RRC/BFQ/ZPath/RoC pillars, MasterP, and relative-rank allocation logic are exactly as they were before this redesign — no formula, weight, or threshold was touched.

Engine C is no longer a prediction engine. It was previously a Bayesian-fusion layer combining Engine A with a second engine ("Engine B," a Shock-Recovery-based behaviour model). A multi-stage walk-forward research program tested that fusion — and, independently, Engine B as a standalone predictor, as an added Engine A pillar, as a position-health template, and as a forward monitoring signal — and found no form of it that survived validation without either failing to beat Engine A alone, or in one case producing a result that was significantly worse than random. Engine B, Bayesian fusion, likelihood ratios, trust-based routing, calibration layers, and confidence formulas were all retired as a direct result of that evidence, not by assumption.

Engine C's job now is strictly translation: it reads Engine A's own output and presents it as a rank (not a probability), a set of historical counts (not a confidence score), an entry verdict (reusing Engine A's own existing thresholds, not a new decision rule), and a descriptive record of an open position (not a health or trend score). Every one of those restrictions reflects a specific finding from the research program, not a stylistic preference.
