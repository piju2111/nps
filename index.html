Engine C — How This System Works

Engine A — Market Structure. Analyzes price action across 8 pillars (Stage, Zone, ZPath, RRC, Weekday, BFQ, WOM, RoC) to measure how "coiled" or "extended" the market structure currently is. Outputs a MasterP score (0-100+, can go negative) and a Regime classification (HighSptR/LowSptR, based on ATR-to-price volatility percentile). Frozen — no formula, weight, or threshold changes without your explicit sign-off.

Engine B — Shock Recovery Behaviour Engine. Evaluates the market's current state, not a library of historical evidence keys. It finds the largest volatility shock inside the trailing 9-day window, measures how far the market has recovered from it, ranks that recovery against its own historical distribution, and converts the resulting Likelihood Ratio straight into a Blast Readiness Index (BRI, 0-100). Frozen — no formula changes.

Engine C — Decision Intelligence (this layer). Never alters Engine A or B's math. It only reads their outputs, compares them, and explains the relationship. This is the sole place where the two engines' signals are combined.

Engine B — Shock Recovery Pipeline

Engine B no longer scores collections of historical evidence keys (the old Quietness/Direction SD/STK/TRN Evidence Library). It evaluates one behaviour — Shock Recovery — through a single, always-on pipeline:

9-day window
  ↓
Largest Shock  (loudest day, by TR/ATR, anywhere in the window)
  ↓
Recovery Score  (how quiet the days after the shock were, relative to the shock itself)
  ↓
Percentile Bucket  (Low / Medium / High, vs. the regime's own historical distribution)
  ↓
Historical Blast/Miss counts  (how that bucket has actually resolved, historically)
  ↓
Laplace smoothing
  ↓
Likelihood Ratio
  ↓
logLR
  ↓
BRI

Shock-age cutoff has been removed. A validation study tested whether shocks should expire after a fixed number of days (or decay in influence with age) and found no statistically significant relationship between shock age and Blast probability, no cutoff that improved both discrimination and calibration, and no evidence favoring decay weighting either. Every valid shock inside the fixed 9-day window is now treated equally, regardless of how old it is — the model evaluates the current recovery state, not the number of days since the shock occurred.

What Each Metric Means

Conviction Score (0-100). The final trading confidence signal. Calculated as the Bayesian Posterior Probability, pulled toward a neutral 50 in proportion to how unreliable the current setup is (low reliability = more pulled toward neutral, not a simple average of A and B).

Blast Probability. The Bayesian Posterior Probability of a volatility blast (2×ATR move) within 10 trading days. Prior = the historical blast rate observed for this exact combination of Engine A signal strength and Engine B's Shock Recovery bucket (from the Decision Matrix). Likelihood = Engine B's own Shock Recovery Likelihood Ratio. Posterior = properly combined via Bayesian odds — not an arbitrary blend.

Agreement. How closely Engine A's signal strength and Engine B's Shock Recovery signal currently match, based on dynamically computed tercile buckets (Low/Medium/High) of each engine's own historical score distribution — never hardcoded thresholds.

Reliability. How much historical data backs today's specific combination of signals. Based on: how many similar historical days exist (sample size, bucketed dynamically), whether Shock Recovery is currently active, and how well A and B agree. Low reliability means today's setup is relatively untested historically.

Suggested Position. Translated from Engine A's dynamic allocation logic, which ranks today's MasterP score against its own regime-specific historical range. "Stand Down" means today ranks too low within its regime's own history to justify a position; a percentage means today ranks at that percentile of the regime's historical range.

Historical Similar Setups. The number of past days that landed in the exact same (Engine A bucket, Engine B Shock Recovery bucket) cell of the Decision Matrix, and what fraction of those actually blasted within 10 days. This is the empirical prior feeding the Bayesian calculation above.

Consistency Validation

The dashboard automatically compares the Historical Blast Rate for today's setup against the final Blast Probability. A large gap between the two is not an error — it usually means the Bayesian update (a fresh Shock Recovery reading from Engine B, or a low-reliability penalty) is meaningfully overriding the raw historical average. When the gap exceeds a configurable threshold, a review banner explains which component is driving the difference, so nothing is presented as a black box.

Frozen Components (v1.0 RC)

Engine A math, Engine B's Shock Recovery pipeline, Bayesian fusion formula, Feature Store logic, and historical cycle generation are all frozen as of this release. Only bug fixes are permitted going forward, unless live trading data provides strong evidence justifying a mathematical change — and that decision is reserved for you.
